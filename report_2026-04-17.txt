  When in a calorie deficit, the body can burn muscle tissue for fuel

7. KEY INSIGHTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

RECOVERY AT 44 -- ARE THE GAPS ADEQUATE?
At 44, muscle protein synthesis (the repair process after training) takes
15-20% longer to complete than at 25. The practical rule: compound
movements targeting the same muscle group need 48-72 hours between
sessions. This week, most gaps were 24h or 48h. The one concern is
S4 Pull (Apr 14) followed by S5 Legs (Apr 15) 23 hours later, with
Romanian DL loading the lower back and traps that were already hit in
Pull. That said, the week's data shows this was manageable -- no
session was skipped, and session durations were normal (61-84 min on
the full sessions). For Week 2, restructure so Pull and Legs are
separated by at least one rest day.

THE REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP -- WHAT HAPPENS LONG-TERM:
The shoulder is held in its socket partly by four rotator cuff muscles,
with the rear deltoid providing critical posterior stability. When
pressing muscles (anterior deltoid, chest) are far stronger than the
rear delt -- as they are here, with Shoulder Press at 93.3kg e1RM vs
Face Pull at 32.6kg e1RM -- the shoulder sits slightly forward in its
socket. Over months, this causes anterior impingement (the tendon gets
pinched under the acromion bone with every press), reduces overhead
range of motion, and eventually results in rotator cuff tears if ignored.
At 44M, tendons have reduced collagen turnover and are more brittle than
at younger ages. Current numbers: 3 rear-delt sets this week vs 8+ target.
The trap gap (7 sets, just at minimum) is less urgent but still means
less scapular [shoulder blade] control and stability over time.
