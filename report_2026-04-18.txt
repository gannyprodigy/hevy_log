  Session times: Mon 08:31, Tue 08:56, Wed 07:58, Fri 08:14 IST (all

  For a 44M on a calorie deficit, morning training (7-9 AM) aligns with

REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP -- SHOULDER HEALTH CONSEQUENCES IN PLAIN TERMS:

The shoulder joint does not sit in a deep socket the way the hip does.
It is held in position almost entirely by soft tissue: four rotator cuff
muscles, the rear deltoid, and the lower trapezius. When the muscles on
the front of the shoulder (anterior deltoid, pectoral minor) are much
stronger than those on the back (rear deltoid, lower trapezius), the
ball of the shoulder joint shifts slightly forward in its socket. Every
overhead press, every lat pulldown, every push movement then pinches the
supraspinatus tendon (a rotator cuff muscle) against the acromion -- the
bony arch at the top of the shoulder. This is anterior impingement. In
the short term it causes a nagging ache at the front of the shoulder
during pressing. In the medium term (3-6 months of uncorrected imbalance)
it frays the tendon. In the long term it tears. At 44, tendon collagen
turnover is roughly 30% slower than at 25, meaning partial tears heal
poorly and incompletely -- and can progress to full tears under continued
load. The current numbers make this risk concrete and quantifiable:
Shoulder Press e1RM = 98.0kg. Face Pull e1RM (best rear delt proxy in
the log) = 32.6kg. That is a 3.0:1 anterior-to-posterior strength ratio.
A healthy shoulder has a ratio of 2:1 or less. At 6 rear delt sets this
week (still below the 8-set minimum), the gap is not widening week over
week but it is not closing meaningfully either. The Rear Delt Reverse Fly
addition (35x12, e1RM 49.0kg) on Friday is the right exercise at the
right load -- but 3 sets once per week is insufficient. The target is
Face Pull and Rear Delt Fly in every session until Face Pull e1RM reaches
at least 45-50kg (currently 32.6kg, meaning roughly a 40% strength gain
is needed to reach a healthy balance).
