WHY THIS MATTERS AT 44 AND ON A DEFICIT:
  When calories are below maintenance, the body needs a reason to keep
  muscle rather than burning it for fuel. That reason is mechanical tension
  (the signal from resistance training) and dietary protein. At 44, anabolic
  hormones that protect muscle (testosterone, IGF-1, growth hormone) are
  15-20% lower than at 30. This means the training signal must be stronger,
  not weaker, to maintain lean mass at the same deficit. Missing sessions or
  underdoing sets for a muscle group removes the mechanical tension signal --
  the body then treats that muscle as unnecessary and catabolises it. For
  Ganesh at 87kg targeting 79kg, approximately 2-3 kg of the 8 kg to lose
  could come from muscle if training and protein are inadequate.

REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP -- SHOULDER HEALTH CONSEQUENCES IN PLAIN TERMS:

The shoulder joint does not sit in a deep socket the way the hip does. It is
held in place almost entirely by soft tissue: the rotator cuff (4 small muscles)
and the surrounding larger muscles including the rear deltoid. When the muscles
on the front of the shoulder (anterior deltoid, pectoral minor) are significantly
stronger than the muscles on the back (rear deltoid, lower trapezius), the
shoulder blade tilts forward and the ball of the shoulder joint migrates
slightly toward the front of the socket. Every overhead press, every lat
pulldown, every push-up then pinches the tendon of the supraspinatus (a rotator
cuff muscle) against a bony arch at the top of the shoulder called the acromion.
This is anterior impingement. In the short term it causes pain at the front of
the shoulder during pressing. In the medium term it frays the tendon. In the
long term it tears it -- and at 44, tendon collagen turnover is roughly 30%
slower than at 25, meaning tears heal poorly and incompletely. The current
numbers make this risk concrete: Shoulder Press e1RM is 98.0kg; Face Pull
e1RM (the best rear delt proxy in the log) is 32.6kg. That is a 3:1 anterior-
to-posterior strength ratio. A healthy ratio is closer to 2:1 or less. At 6
rear delt sets this week (target 8+), the gap is not widening -- but it is
not closing either. The Rear Delt Fly addition on Friday (35x12, e1RM 49.0kg)
is the right exercise, done at the right load, but 3 sets once a week is
insufficient. Target is Face Pull and Rear Delt Fly in every session until
the Face Pull e1RM reaches at least 45kg (currently 32.6kg).
