7. KEY INSIGHTS
========================================================================

  RECOVERY AT 44
  At 44, muscle protein synthesis takes longer to peak and cortisol stays
  elevated further into the recovery window than it did in your 20s and 30s.
  Training the same muscle before it has recovered (less than 48h) adds
  accumulated fatigue rather than productive stimulus, and repeated short-
  cycling is one of the top causes of overuse tendon injury in masters lifters.
  This week showed two violations: Mon Push -> Tue Pull only 24h apart
  (biceps overlap), and Tue Pull -> Wed Legs only 24h apart (biceps overlap
  via Seated Leg Curl). Both pairs hit below the 48h minimum. Over a single
  week the effect is mild; over months it compounds into stagnation or injury.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  Traps are receiving 10 sets per week (4 above target). Rear delts are at
  7 sets (1 short of the 8-set minimum). This matters because the deltoid's
  three heads must stay balanced: heavy pressing and shrugging loads the
  anterior and medial heads heavily, but the posterior head (rear delt) is
  only worked by horizontal pulls and specific isolation moves. When rear
  delts fall behind, the shoulder capsule is pulled forward under load,
  creating anterior impingement and — over months — rotator cuff fraying.
  Currently one extra rear-delt set per week closes the gap. Face Pull is
  already in the programme and improving (+3.7kg e1RM this week); simply
  keep the 4-set version every Pull session to stay ahead.
