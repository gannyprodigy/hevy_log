  RECOVERY AT 44
  At 44, muscle protein synthesis takes 48-72 hours to complete after a hard
  set — compared to roughly 36 hours in your 20s. Training the same muscle
  before it has repaired adds fatigue on top of damage, not new stimulus, and
  raises soft-tissue injury risk considerably. This week one pair of sessions
  breached that window: Tuesday Pull (biceps via Hammer Curl and Seated Leg
  Curl muscle tagging) ran into Wednesday Legs only 24 hours later — the
  Seated Leg Curl tags biceps as a working muscle. Everything else was fine:
  the Sat→Mon gap was 41h, Mon→Tue was 24h (different muscle groups: Push vs
  Pull), and Wed→Fri was 48h. The fix is simple: shift Wednesday Legs to
  Thursday each week to restore that 48h buffer.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP HEALTH
  When the front shoulder (anterior deltoid and pec) is significantly stronger
  than the rear deltoid and lower trap, the shoulder joint is pulled forward
  under load — this is how rotator cuff impingement and labral irritation
  develop. This week rear delts landed at exactly 8 sets (minimum needed) and
  traps at 10 sets (well above the 6-set floor). That is the correct balance.
  The concern going forward is chest: at 9 sets vs 12 target it is training the
  front of the shoulder more than the back over a multi-week block. Maintain
  8+ rear delt sets every week without fail, and close the chest gap by adding
  sets rather than dropping rear-delt work.
