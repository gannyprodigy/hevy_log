  RECOVERY AT 44
  Muscle protein synthesis at 44 takes 48-72h to complete — longer than in your 20s-30s
  due to lower anabolic hormone levels and slower cellular repair. This week showed two
  back-to-back pairs with only 24h between sessions: Tue 21->Wed 22 (biceps overlap) and
  Fri 24->Sat 25 (back overlap). The Fri/Sat pair is the more consequential one: back is
  already short at 12 sets vs a 14-set target, and deadlifting on Saturday with a fatigued
  posterior chain from Friday's Upper session means you were accumulating fatigue rather
  than adding stimulus. Aim for at least 48h between any sessions sharing a major compound
  muscle group — this is harder to recover from at 44 than it was a decade ago.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP BALANCE
  When the anterior deltoid and upper chest are significantly stronger than the rear
  deltoids and rotator cuff, the shoulder joint is pulled forward in its socket. Over
  months this causes impingement — a sharp pinching sensation under the shoulder blade
  during pressing or overhead movements. This week's numbers are borderline safe: rear
  delts hit exactly the 8-set floor, and the push:pull ratio is 1.67 (OK but drifting
  high). Traps are well-covered at 11 sets. The structural risk is this: any missed Pull
  session tips rear delts below maintenance minimum immediately. Treat Face Pulls and
  Rear Delt Reverse Flyes as non-negotiable — never skip them to save time.
