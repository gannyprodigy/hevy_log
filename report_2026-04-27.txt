  not fresh stimulus — is the likely explanation for Bent Over Row
  dropping -2.0kg e1RM this week despite everything else trending up.

  REAR DELTS AND TRAP BALANCE:
  When front-shoulder muscles (anterior delts, pecs) significantly
  outpace the rear delts, the shoulder blade is pulled forward, narrowing
  the subacromial space and raising impingement and rotator-cuff risk.
  This week the numbers are healthy: rear delts hit exactly their 8-set
  minimum, the pull:push ratio is 1.67 (well above the 1.0 danger line),
  and Rear Delt Reverse Fly posted a strong +7.0kg e1RM gain. The risk
  to watch is any future week where chest or front-shoulder volume spikes
  while rear-delt rows fall below 6 sets — that imbalance accumulates
  silently and shows up as shoulder pain rather than strength loss.

  RED FLAGS THIS WEEK:
  Three specific issues stand out. First, Pull Ups were logged as 0kg in
  both Tuesday sessions (14 Apr and 21 Apr) — this is a bodyweight
  exercise and needs actual bodyweight (79kg) entered so e1RM can be
