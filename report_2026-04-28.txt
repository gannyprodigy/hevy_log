  via seated leg curl), and Friday Upper Body and Saturday Lower Body were 24 hours

  soft-tissue injury risk. Next week: shift Saturday Lower to Sunday so the back
  gets a full 48 hours after Friday's lat pulldown.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP BALANCE
  When chest and anterior (front) shoulder muscles are far stronger than the rear
  deltoids, the scapula gets pulled forward — producing rounded shoulders, restricted
  range of motion, and shoulder impingement that can sideline you for weeks at a time.
  This week's numbers are actually protective in this regard: rear delts logged 12 sets
  (target 8) and traps 15 sets (target 6), both comfortably above minimum. The risk
  currently sits on the other side: chest recorded only 3 sets vs a 12-set target and
  overall shoulder volume was just 3 sets. If the anterior chain keeps falling behind,
  the rotator cuff takes compensatory strain. Maintain the strong rear delt and trap
  work, but close the chest and shoulder gap urgently.
