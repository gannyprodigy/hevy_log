  RECOVERY AT 44
  At 44, muscle protein synthesis stays elevated for 48-72h after a hard
  session — shorter than at 25. Training the same muscle before that window
  closes adds fatigue on top of fatigue, not extra stimulus. This week the
  data shows one violation: Friday Upper (Lat Pulldown, heavy back work)
  was followed 24h later by Saturday Lower (Trap Bar Deadlift, equally
  heavy back work). That is too close. The other 24h gap — Mon Pull into
  Tue Push — is fine because the muscle groups do not overlap.
  Recommendation: move Friday Upper to Thursday, giving 48h before
  Saturday Lower.

  REAR DELTS AND TRAPS
  When the front shoulder muscles (anterior delt, upper chest) are
  disproportionately stronger than the rear (rear delt, lower trap), the
  shoulder blade tilts forward, compressing the rotator cuff tendons with
  every press. Over time this causes impingement and tears. Right now the
  numbers look acceptable: rear delts hit exactly 8 sets (the 8-per-week
  minimum), push:pull ratio is 1.60, and traps are well above target at
  11 sets. The risk is that chest and shoulders are still below target
  (10 vs 12, 9 vs 10), so if those gaps are filled with more push work
  without matching rear-delt sets, the balance will tip. Keep Face Pulls
  and Rear Delt Reverse Flys anchored in every upper session.
