  RECOVERY AT 44
  At 44, muscle protein synthesis takes 48-72h to complete — shorter windows
  don't allow full repair, meaning the next session adds fatigue on top of
  damage rather than building new tissue. This week the data shows one clear
  breach: Friday 24 Apr (Upper Body) was followed by Saturday 25 Apr (Lower
  Body) just 24h later, and both sessions hit back — Shrugs and Trap-bar
  Deadlift on Saturday overlapped with the upper body load on Friday. The
  three remaining gaps (Mon→Tue, Tue→Wed) were also only 24h, acceptable for
  different muscle groups but tight for a 44-year-old. One rest day between
  any sessions sharing a muscle group is the minimum; two is better.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP BALANCE
  When the front-of-shoulder muscles (chest press e1RM 82kg, seated shoulder
  press e1RM 102.5kg) are far stronger than the rear deltoids (rear delt fly
  e1RM 56kg, face pull e1RM 36.3kg), the humeral head is pulled forward in the
  socket. Over time this compresses the rotator cuff tendons, causing
  impingement and increasing shoulder injury risk — particularly on pressing
  movements. This week rear delts hit exactly 8 sets, which meets the minimum
  target, but the strength gap is real. Consistent rear-delt and face-pull work
  is the primary structural protection for Ganesh's shoulders.
