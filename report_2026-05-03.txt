  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  When the muscles that push forward (chest, front deltoid) are much
  stronger than the muscles that pull the shoulder blades back (rear
  delts), the head of the humerus sits slightly forward in the socket.
  Over time this creates impingement — pinching of tendons with every
  overhead or pressing movement. The current numbers show traps are
  well-covered at 11 sets (target 6), and rear delts hit exactly the
  minimum 8 sets this week. That's the floor — not a margin. Any week
  where rear delt sets drop below 8 starts eroding shoulder stability.
  Keep all 4 Face Pull sets every Pull session and all 4 Rear Delt Fly
  sets every Upper session; do not cut these when short on time.
