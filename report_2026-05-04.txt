  When push muscles (front delts, chest) are significantly stronger or more
  trained than the rear of the shoulder, the shoulder blade tilts forward and
  the rotator cuff impinges under load — the most common cause of chronic gym
  shoulder pain. This week: rear delts hit exactly 8 sets (target 8) and traps
  reached 11 sets (target 6). The push:pull ratio is 1.42, which is in the
  healthy range. Shoulder health picture is currently solid. The only task is
  maintaining rear delt work at 8+ sets every week even when sessions are
  short — these are the sets that protect the joint, not build the mirror muscle.
