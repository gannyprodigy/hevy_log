7. KEY INSIGHTS
========================================================================

  RECOVERY AT 44
  At 44, muscle protein synthesis after a hard set takes 48-72h to complete. Training
  the same muscle before that window closes adds fatigue on top of incomplete repair —
  you get diminishing returns from the session and raise injury risk. This week showed
  two recovery violations: Fri 01 May + Sat 02 May were only 24h apart with back overlap,
  and Sat 02 May + Mon 04 May were 48h apart with back, core, and trap overlap. The back
  in particular took three sessions in five days without a full rest window between any
  consecutive pair. Rearranging so that at least 48h separates sessions that share a
  major muscle group will produce better strength gains with less cumulative wear.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP BALANCE
  The front of the shoulder (anterior delt, chest) gets heavy work every Push session.
  The rear delt and rotator cuff stabilisers get far less. When the front muscles are
  substantially stronger than the rear, the shoulder joint is pulled forward out of
  neutral — this is a common mechanism for rotator cuff impingement and biceps tendon
  irritation. This week: rear delts reached 7 sets against a target of 8, a small but
  recurring gap. Traps are fine at 10 sets (target 6). The rear delt gap is manageable
  now but needs consistent weekly attention. Face pulls and reverse flys are the fix —
  both are already in the programme; aim for 3+ sets per Pull or Upper session.

     sets jumped to 90kg for only 8 reps — a 4-rep drop. Jumping 10kg and losing 4 reps
     in the same session suggests the warm-up weight was heavier than intended, leaving

  2. Pull Up logged at 0kg (Mon 04 May): bodyweight pull-ups record 0kg by default in

  3. Triceps Pushdown weight discrepancy: the Cult gym cable stack produced 24.9kg in

     These are different machines with different cable ratios — not a strength loss. Use
