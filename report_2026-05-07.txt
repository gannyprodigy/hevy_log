  At 44, muscle tissue repairs more slowly — the evidence base points to 48–72 h
  between training bouts on the same muscle group for full recovery. This week
  showed three problematic gaps: Friday Upper Body (lat pulldown, rear delts) was
  followed only 24 h later by Saturday deadlifts (back, traps). Monday Pull and
  Tuesday Push were 25 h apart, and Tuesday-to-Wednesday was just 23 h. None of
  these are catastrophic in a single week, but stacking compressed gaps across five
  consecutive sessions compounds fatigue rather than adding training stimulus. The
  one positive signal: no sessions after midnight all week.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  When the anterior deltoid and upper chest are meaningfully stronger than the rear
  delts and external rotators, the shoulder joint is pulled slightly forward at rest.
  Over time this strains the rotator cuff. This week rear delts logged only 7 sets —
  one short of the 8-set minimum. Traps are already over-covered at 13 sets (target 6).
  The fix is not more trap work; it is consistently hitting 8+ rear-delt sets each
  week. Add one extra face pull or reverse fly set every Pull session to close the
  gap reliably.

     then the working sets jumped to 90 kg x 8. A 12.5% load increase paired with a
     33% rep drop in the same session signals that either the warm-up was logged as a
     working set, or the jump was too aggressive. Next session: hold 90 kg and drive

     The system flagged a "weight drop" — confirm which cable station you use at each

     For pull-ups, add a +5 kg plate as soon as you can hit 3 × 8 bodyweight; this
