  training the same muscle before that window closes adds fatigue rather than
  stimulus. This week had two problematic overlaps: Friday (Upper / back via
  Lat Pulldown) into Saturday (Lower / back via Trap-bar Deadlift) with only
  24h between sessions; and Saturday into Monday at exactly 48h, again with
  back, traps and core shared. The 24h Friday-Saturday gap is the bigger
  concern — the back and traps had no meaningful recovery before being loaded
  again. Structurally, Push (Tue) and Legs (Wed) sitting only 23h apart is
  fine because the muscle groups don't overlap, but the cumulative CNS load of
  five sessions in six days is still high.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  When the front of the shoulder (anterior deltoid, pecs) is consistently
  stronger than the rear (posterior deltoid, rotator cuff), the humeral head
  is pulled forward in the socket. Over time this compresses the supraspinatus
  tendon and increases impingement risk — a common and often permanent injury
  in older lifters. This week: rear delts hit 7 sets (target 8+), which is
  only 1 set short and manageable. Traps are well covered at 13 sets. The
  pull:push ratio of 1.58 is healthy. The action item is simply to protect the
  one rear-delt set gap — add one extra set of face pulls or reverse flys to
  any upper or pull session next week.

  RED FLAGS THIS WEEK

     (previous) to 90kg x 8. The e1RM improved (+7.3kg to 114kg) but the
     session started at 80kg x 12 and the working sets dropped to 8 reps —

     slightly too aggressive. Hold at 90kg x 8 next session before moving up.
  2. Triceps Pushdown weight discrepancy (55kg Fri vs 27.2kg Tue): These two
