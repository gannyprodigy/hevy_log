7. KEY INSIGHTS
========================================================================

  RECOVERY AT 44
  At 44, muscle tissue needs 48-72 hours to complete protein synthesis and repair
  micro-tears before it can be effectively loaded again. Training a muscle in the
  repair window adds fatigue rather than stimulus and raises injury risk. This week
  had two close consecutive sessions: Mon 04 May (Pull — back, traps, rear delts,
  biceps) followed 25h later by Tue 05 May (Push — chest, shoulders, triceps). Those
  sessions hit largely different muscle groups, so the gap is acceptable. However,
  Sat 02 May (Lower — back, traps, core) was followed only 48h later by Mon 04 May
  (Pull — back, traps), placing back and traps at the low end of the recovery window.
  Over 4 weeks this is manageable; consistently training back/traps under 48h apart
  will accumulate fatigue and eventually degrade strength.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP BALANCE
  The shoulder joint is stabilised by a ring of muscles: anterior (front) deltoid,
  lateral deltoid, and — critically — rear deltoid and rotator cuff. When the front
  and lateral fibres dominate because they get more volume, the humeral head is
  pulled forward in the socket. Over months this causes impingement, rotator cuff
  fraying, and AC joint pain. This week traps are well above target (13 sets vs 6
  needed) but rear delts came in at 7 sets vs a target of 8 — a small but persistent
  gap. Rear delts received work through Face Pulls (3 sets) and Rear Delt Reverse
  Fly (4 sets on Friday). Adding one extra set of face pulls on any session brings
  this into compliance without adding meaningful fatigue.
