  Sat Lower with leg-only work.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  When the muscles that push your arms forward (anterior deltoids, pectorals)
  are significantly stronger than those that pull them back (rear deltoids,
  rotator cuff), the shoulder sits too far forward in the socket. Over time this
  causes impingement and rotator cuff tears — a particular risk after 40. This
  week rear delts got 7 sets against a target of 8, short by 1. Traps are
  healthy at 9 sets (target 6). The pull:push ratio of 1.38 is balanced overall,
  but rear delts specifically need one extra face pull or reverse fly every week.
  This gap has persisted for multiple weeks — it must be built into the session
  plan, not treated as optional.
