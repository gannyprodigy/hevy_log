  window at 48-72h between sessions that load the same muscle group.
  This week four of the five gaps were fine: the Mon→Tue (25h) and
  Tue→Wed (23h) transitions hit different muscle groups, and the
  Wed→Fri (49h) gap gave legs a full rest. The problem is Fri 08 May
  → Sat 09 May (24h). Friday Upper included Lat Pulldown and Bicep
  Curl; Saturday Lower opened with Trap-bar Deadlift, which also
  hammers back and traps. Repeating back with under 48h of recovery
  means the session adds fatigue rather than new stimulus, and the
  neuromuscular system is still suppressed. Move the Saturday Lower
  session to Sunday, or swap Friday Upper to upper-push only and keep
  all back work on Pull day.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  When the muscles on the front of the shoulder (chest, front delt,
  triceps) are substantially stronger than the rear delts, the
  humerus is pulled forward in its socket. Over time this narrows the
  subacromial space, compresses the rotator cuff tendons, and creates
  the classic "impingement" pattern — pain on overhead pressing and
  lateral raises. The minimum to prevent this drift is 8 rear-delt
  sets per week; this week was 7 (1 set short). Traps are fine at 9
  vs a 6-set target. One extra set of Face Pulls or Reverse Flys on
  any session brings rear delts back into balance.
