  to Sat 09 May was only 24 hours apart, with back and biceps appearing in both
  sessions (Lat Pulldown / Rear Delt on Fri; Deadlift on Sat both load the back).
  The Sat 09 May to Mon 11 May gap was 48 hours — borderline, hitting back,
  biceps, core and traps again. Two consecutive weeks of 5 sessions is positive,
  but stacking the back twice within 24 hours adds cumulative fatigue, not stimulus.
  Next week, move Saturday's lower session to Sunday or push Friday's upper to
  Thursday to open a full 48h window between back-loading days.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  When the front and side shoulder muscles (anterior / lateral delts) are
  significantly stronger than the rear delts, the shoulder joint is pulled
  forward out of neutral. Over time this compresses the rotator cuff tendons,
  causing impingement or tears — common in 40+ lifters who push heavy without
  matching their rear-delt work. This week: rear delts got 7 sets against a
  target of 8. The gap is small (1 set) but consistent. Traps are fine at 9
  sets. Adding a single extra set of face pulls or reverse flys to any session
  closes this every week at negligible cost.
