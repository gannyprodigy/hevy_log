  REAR DELTS AND SHOULDER HEALTH
  When the chest and front shoulders significantly outpace the rear delts,
  the humeral head migrates forward in the socket. That imbalance loads the
  rotator cuff tendons in a compromised position, which is the most common
  cause of shoulder impingement in gym-goers over 40. This week rear delts
  reached only 7 sets vs the 8-set weekly minimum, while the front-of-body
  push muscles (chest + shoulders) accumulated 29 sets. The gap is small
  but consistent: rear delts have been the most persistently underfunded
  muscle group across the 30-day log.
