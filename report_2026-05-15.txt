7. KEY INSIGHTS
========================================================================

  RECOVERY AT 44:
  Muscles in a 44-year-old need 48-72 hours to fully repair — not 24. Satellite
  cell activity slows with age, protein synthesis rates drop, and inflammation
  clears more slowly than in your 20s. This week, three session pairs were
  separated by only 24 hours: Fri/Sat (back + biceps overlap), Mon/Tue (minor
  push/pull shoulder overlap), and Wed/Thu (back overlap). The Fri/Sat pair is
  the most problematic — Lat Pulldown on Friday and Deadlift on Saturday are
  both heavy back-dominant movements. Hitting the same muscle before it has
  recovered adds accumulated fatigue, not new stimulus, and raises injury risk.
  Target at least 48h before a second heavy session on the same muscle group.

  REAR DELT AND TRAP HEALTH:
  When the front-facing shoulder muscles (anterior deltoid, pec minor, upper
  traps) outpace the rear-facing ones (posterior deltoid, lower traps, rhomboids),
  the shoulder blade tilts forward — a pattern called upper-crossed syndrome.
  Over time this impinges the rotator cuff and causes chronic shoulder pain. The
  good news: 11 rear-delt sets this week (target 8+) and 9 trap sets (target 6+)
  are both comfortably above threshold. The pull:push ratio at 1.21 is healthy.
  Maintain this balance as loads increase on the pressing movements.

  RED FLAGS THIS WEEK:
  (1) Recovery crunch: Fri 08 May + Sat 09 May = only 24h gap with back/biceps
  overlap. Wed 13 May + Thu 14 May = only 24h gap with back overlap. Accumulating
  these 24h turnarounds week after week is the most likely injury vector at 44.
  (2) Triceps Pushdown e1RM collapsed -39.8kg on Tue 12 May (27.2kg at Cult vs
  60kg at Drona). These are different machines/stacks — the number is not a real
  strength drop but the logging inconsistency masks true progress. Consider noting
  the gym name in exercise notes going forward.
  (3) Romanian Deadlift down -3.8kg e1RM vs the prior week (40kg x 11 vs 45kg x 9
  four weeks ago). Combined with the 0kg bodyweight entries for Pull Up, Sissy Squat,
  and Dead Bug, there are 3 exercises with no trackable load progression this week.
