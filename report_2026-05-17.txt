  REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP:
  The shoulder is a ball-and-socket joint stabilised by a force couple: front
  deltoid and chest push the arm forward; rear delts, infraspinatus and lower
  traps pull it back into the socket. When pushing muscles are consistently
  stronger than pulling muscles the humeral head migrates anterior in the socket,
  grinding into the rotator cuff tendons — a direct pathway to impingement and
  tears. This week rear delts logged 7 sets vs the 8/week target (1 set short).
  Traps are well covered at 13 sets vs 6 target. The fix is simple: add one
  extra set of face pulls or reverse flys every Monday Pull session.
