7. KEY INSIGHTS
========================================================================

  RECOVERY AT 44
  At 44 years old, muscle tissue needs 48-72 hours to fully rebuild
  between sessions targeting the same muscle group. This week's data
  shows three consecutive days hitting back: Wed 13 May (Romanian
  Deadlift, a back-dominant compound), Thu 14 May (Upper Body Drona
  with Lat Pulldown, Rear Delt Fly, and direct back work), and Fri 15
  May (Trap Bar Deadlift). These sessions were only 24-25h apart. On a
  calorie deficit, training an under-recovered muscle adds cumulative
  fatigue rather than new stimulus — and at 44, recovery hormones
  (testosterone, IGF-1, HGH) are lower than at 25, making this more
  consequential. Rearranging sessions to place at least one full rest
  day between back-heavy workouts is the single highest-leverage
  scheduling fix available.

  THE REAR DELT AND TRAP GAP
  When front-chain muscles (chest, anterior deltoid, triceps) are
  significantly stronger than the rear-chain (rear delts, external
  rotators), the humeral head is pulled forward in the socket under
  load. Over years this causes impingement, rotator cuff fraying, and
  eventually structural damage — the kind that needs surgery. The current
  numbers show rear delts at 7 sets this week against an 8-set target.
  While 1 set short sounds minor, it is a consistent shortfall that
  compounds over months. Traps are well above target (13 vs 6) so trap
  health is fine. The fix is simple: make Face Pulls and Rear Delt Fly
  the last non-negotiable sets of every Pull and Upper Body session
  before anything gets cut.

  RED FLAGS THIS WEEK
  1. Triceps Pushdown showed -44.8kg e1RM vs prior (37.2kg this week
     vs ~82kg in prior Upper Body sessions). This is not a strength
     regression — it is two different exercises sharing the same name.
     The Cult sessions use a lighter cable attachment (27.2kg stack)
     while the Drona Upper Body session uses a heavier cable. These
     should be logged separately in Hevy to prevent false regression
     alerts.
  2. Cable Crunch: Set 1 logged at 35kg then dropped to 30kg for Sets 2
     and 3 in the same session (Mon 11 May). A 14% mid-set weight drop
     suggests the first set was too heavy or the weight was misloaded.
     Keep at 30kg next session and build steadily.
  3. Three consecutive back-heavy sessions (Wed-Thu-Fri) with only 24-25h
     gaps — flagged again here as it is the most structurally significant
     issue in this week's data.
