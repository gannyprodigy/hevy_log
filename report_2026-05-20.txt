  Body (Lat Pulldown) and Wednesday Legs (Romanian Deadlift) session loaded the
  back, so the same musculature was called to work again before the prior session
  had cleared. Running stimulus ahead of recovery converts productive training into
  cumulative fatigue and elevates injury risk—especially in the lower back and
  shoulder girdle. The abbreviated Monday session (42 min) almost certainly reflects
  that accumulated fatigue. Rearranging Thursday to Friday and Friday to Saturday
  restores the 48h buffer without losing a session.

  Rear delts and trap gap: Shoulder joint stability depends on a balanced ratio
  between the front deltoid/pec group and the rear deltoid/external rotator group.
  This week the front-shoulder pressing e1RM sits around 106kg (Seated Shoulder
  Press Machine) while the rear delt indicator (Rear Delt Reverse Fly) tops out at
  68kg—a 56% gap. When anterior muscles are substantially stronger, the humeral
  head migrates forward in the socket, and over time this leads to rotator cuff
  impingement and labral wear. At 7 rear delt sets against an 8-set target the
  weekly gap is only 1 set, but the structural imbalance warrants consistent rear
  delt priority in every session—Face Pulls and Reverse Flys should never be
  dropped to fit time. Traps (13 sets vs 6 target) are in excellent shape.
