  REAR DELTS VS FRONT SHOULDER — WHY THIS GAP IS A SLOW INJURY
  The rear deltoid and rotator-cuff muscles act as the anchor that keeps the head of
  the humerus sitting squarely in its socket. When the front-of-shoulder muscles
  (chest, front delt) are consistently stronger, they pull the shoulder joint forward.
  Over months that wears on the bicep tendon and rotator cuff — the injury that ends
  many training careers. This week: 7 rear-delt sets vs the 8-set minimum. Traps are
  fine at 10 sets. The fix is simple — add one extra set of Face Pulls or Reverse Fly
  every Pull session.
