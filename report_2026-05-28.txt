  CORRECTIVE COMPLIANCE

  Hip Thrust was absent from Legs A (Wed 27 May). The Glute Bridge (0kg,
  bodyweight) was present but does not substitute for loaded Hip Thrust on the
  machine. Hip Thrust is the primary corrective for anterior pelvic tilt (APT):
  the loaded hip extension pattern teaches the pelvis to posteriorly rotate
  under tension. Without it, the pelvis stays tilted forward, shifting load onto
  the lumbar spine during all squat and deadlift movements and reducing glute
  activation by up to 30%. This is a hard miss -- Legs A Wed 27 May had no APT
  correction. Rear delt sets this week total 6 (3 from Face Pull in Push A + 3
  from Face Pull in Pull A), which is 2 short of the 8-set weekly target. At
  age 44, if the anterior deltoid consistently outloads the rear delt, the
  humeral head migrates forward in the shoulder socket during pressing. This
  compresses the subacromial space and loads the supraspinatus tendon -- a
  mechanism for rotator cuff impingement that becomes chronic and difficult to
  train around. Adding even 2 rear delt sets per Pull session closes this gap.
  Core stabiliser compliance was excellent: Bird Dog in Push A and Pull A, Dead
  Bug plus Side Plank in Legs A -- 3/3 sessions covered the T8-T12 corrective.
