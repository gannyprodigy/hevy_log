traps via Pull Up (Weighted), Dumbbell Row, and Rack Pull. Wed 27 May (Legs A) then loaded the

muscles were then partially stressed again when Thu 28 May (Push B) included Upright Row (cable,
23h after the Deadlift). Two back-to-back recovery violations on the same muscle group (back/traps)
across Tue->Wed->Thu. At 44, where protein synthesis rates after training are measurably slower
than in younger trainees, repeating load before the previous session has resolved is adding
accumulated fatigue rather than a new growth signal. A simple fix: shift Legs A to Thursday and
Push B to Friday so there is a clear day of separation for back between Pull A and the next
back-loading session.

This is Week 1 of the PPL x2 programme (started 2026-05-24). Adherence sits at 67% -- 4 of 6
sessions completed. All four logged sessions were on the correct PPL day, which is encouraging.
The pattern driving the miss is clear: Mon-Tue-Wed-Thu done, then Friday (Pull B) and Saturday
(Legs B) skipped. Two possible causes: fatigue accumulation from four consecutive days, or

the cause, moving to a Mon/Wed/Fri + Tue/Thu/Sat pattern in Week 2 would distribute load better
and remove the consecutive-day recovery violations described above.

CORRECTIVE COMPLIANCE
Hip Thrust hit rate this week: 0 of 1 Legs sessions. Hip Thrust is mandatory for anterior pelvic
tilt (APT) correction. APT shortens the hip flexors and chronically under-activates the glutes;
Hip Thrust is the direct counter-stimulus. One missed Legs session without it is one week without
any corrective signal on the APT. Rear delt sets this week: 9 of 8 target -- technically met, but
it is worth understanding why the target is hard-set at 8+. When the front-delt muscles (anterior
deltoid, trained via OHP and lateral raise compensatory anterior loading) accumulate significantly
more total volume than the rear delts and external rotators, the humeral head is pulled anteriorly
in the joint socket during pressing movements. At 44, rotator cuff tendons have reduced collagen
turnover capacity, so even modest impingement load over weeks can escalate to a tear that sidelines
training entirely. 9 rear-delt sets this week is the minimum floor, not a target to stay exactly at.
Core stabiliser presence: 4 of 4 sessions with Bird Dog -- good T8-T12 compliance.

LATERAL RAISE TREND -- V-TAPER KPI
This week: 10kg x 15 reps. Last available prior: 12.5kg x 13 reps (Sat 16 May). Trend: DOWN.
Weight has dropped 2.5kg. For Ganesh, whose love handles sit at approximately the same horizontal

deltoid development. Wider delts make the waist look narrower without any change in actual waist
circumference. A downward trend in lateral raise load means less mechanical tension on the medial
deltoid head -- the exact fibre responsible for the width increase needed. The 10kg -> 12.5kg step
is the priority progression in Week 2.
