CORRECTIVE COMPLIANCE:
Hip Thrust hit rate this week: 0 of 1 Legs sessions (0%). The Legs A session on
27 May included Glute Bridge and Clamshell as warm-ups but no Hip Thrust
whatsoever. The Hip Thrust trains the posterior pelvic tilters (glutes) under
axial load -- this is the direct counter-stimulus to the hip flexor dominance
driving the Anterior Pelvic Tilt (APT). A Glute Bridge is a useful activation
exercise but it does not replace the Hip Thrust as a strength movement; the
loading is too low to generate the posterior pelvic tilt stimulus needed for
correction. Core stabiliser presence: 2 of 3 sessions (67%) -- present in Legs
A and Push B, absent in the Full Body session. Rear delt count this week: 3 sets
vs the 8-set weekly target (5 sets short). All 3 rear delt sets came from one
Face Pull exercise in Push B -- no Pull session was completed. At 44, weak rear
delts allow the anterior deltoid and pec minor to pull the humeral head forward
in the shoulder socket under pressing load. This places the supraspinatus tendon
in a compressed position under the acromion arch. Repeated over months of heavy
pressing with imbalanced anterior/posterior delt strength, this is the primary
mechanism behind rotator cuff impingement and tears in men training in their 40s.
Closing the rear delt gap is not optional -- it is an injury prevention deadline.
