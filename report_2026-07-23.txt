CORRECTIVE COMPLIANCE
Hip thrust hit rate this week: 0% -- both Legs A (Wed) and Legs B (Sat) were skipped
entirely. Hip Thrust is mandatory to address anterior pelvic tilt (APT). APT means
the pelvis tilts forward, causing the hip flexors to stay chronically tight and the
glutes to underfire. Over time this compresses the lumbar discs during compound
lifts and flattens the glute profile. Missing both Legs days means zero APT work for
the entire week.

Rear delt sets: 2 this week versus the 8-set weekly target -- 25% of target. This
matters more at 44 than it might look: every set of chest press and shoulder press
strengthens the anterior deltoid. The rear delt is the posterior counterbalance. When
front shoulder muscles remain significantly stronger than rear delts, the humeral head
(ball of the shoulder joint) is pulled forward in the socket under load. This is the
exact mechanism of rotator cuff impingement -- the tendons of the rotator cuff get
pinched between the humeral head and the acromion. Impingement at 44 often means
months off training, not days.

Core stabiliser: 0 of 3 sessions contained a Dead Bug or Bird-Dog -- the specific
movements that resist excessive spinal extension at T8-T12. Machine Crunch appeared
once (Fri) but trains trunk flexion rather than anti-extension. Without Dead Bug or
Bird-Dog, the lower thoracic kyphosis correction is receiving zero direct work.
